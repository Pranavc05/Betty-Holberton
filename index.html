Frances Elizabeth "Betty" Holberton

Pioneer in Computer Programming

Honoring a Computing Pioneer

This website celebrates Betty Holberton, one of the six original programmers of the ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer), the world's first general-purpose electronic digital computer. Born in 1917 and passing in 2001, Holberton was a trailblazer who helped shape modern computing despite facing significant gender barriers in her field.

Through her work, she demonstrated that computing was not just about hardwareâ€”it was about logical problem-solving, programming languages, and human-computer interaction. Her contributions laid the foundation for technologies we use every day.

Quick Facts

  • Born: March 7, 1917, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
  • Died: December 8, 2001, Maryland
  • Key Achievement: One of six original ENIAC programmers
  • Awards: Ada Lovelace Award (1997), Women in Technology Hall of Fame
  • Legacy: Co-developed COBOL, designed keyboard layouts, innovated in user interfaces
